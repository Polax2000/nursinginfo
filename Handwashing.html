PROPER HANDWASHING TECHNIQUE



What is Handwashing?

According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, maintaining proper hand hygiene is essential to reducing the transmission of infections.

In fact, studies have found that washing your hands can reduce your risk of contracting specific respiratory and gastrointestinal infections by up to 23 and 48 percent, respectively.

Washing your hands frequently is crucial, according to the CDC, to halt the transmission of COVID-19, an illness caused by the novel coronavirus known as SARS-CoV-2.

Steps in Proper Handwashing


Step 1: Wet your hands with clean — preferably running — water.

Step 2: Apply enough soap to cover all surfaces of your hands and wrists.

Step 3: Lather and rub your hands together briskly and thoroughly. Make sure to scrub all surfaces of your hands, fingertips, fingernails, and wrists.

Step 4: Scrub your hands and wrists for at least 20 seconds.

Step 5: Rinse your hands and wrists under clean — preferably running — water.

Step 6: Dry your hands and wrists with a clean towel, or let them air-dry..

Step 7: Use a towel to turn off the faucet.
